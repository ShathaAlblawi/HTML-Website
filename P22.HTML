Read about the planet Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant with an average radius about nine-and-a-half times that of Earth. Despite its low density, Saturn is over 95 times more massive than Earth. The planet is famous for its beautiful rings, visible through a telescope. It takes approximately 29 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun and rotates on its axis in about 10 hours and 32 minutes. Watch a video about Saturn

Read about the planet Neptune

Neptune is the third-largest planet in the Solar System and the eighth planet from the Sun. Since Pluto's reclassification as a dwarf planet in 2006, Neptune holds the title of the farthest planet from the Sun. It has a mass approximately 17 times that of Earth, making it more massive than Uranus. Due to its great distance, Neptune cannot be seen with the naked eye and appears as a small, pale blue disk through a telescope. Its blue color is caused by methane gas, which absorbs red light from the Sun. Watch a video about Neptune

Read about planet Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun, following Mercury and Venus. It is the only planet known to support life and is the fifth-largest in the Solar System. With diverse ecosystems and abundant water, Earth provides a suitable environment for millions of species, including humans.
